📋 Daily Procedures & Protocols
🌅 Morning Procedures
1 Start Computer — Log in. Open Chrome. Log in to Gmail: reception@hartdistrict.org / Cover4me321
2 Open the Gate — Press the OPEN button next to the door by the light switch
3 Set Up Desk — Place the sign-in sheet on the counter
4 Turn Off Call Forwarding — Press Forward Off on the phone
🍽️ Lunch Break (12:00 PM – 1:00 PM)
1 Coverage — Angelica will typically cover your break
2 Left Button (under counter, left corner) — Opens doors to back lobby (press & release)
3 Right Button — Opens HR door (press and hold)
🌙 End-of-Day Procedures
1 Sign-In Sheet — Place clipboard next to file holder. If covering multiple days, file the sheet and set up a blank one.
2 Shut Down — Turn off computer. Close the gate (press CLOSE button).
3 Call Forwarding — Press Forward All, then Voicemail Button to forward calls overnight.
📞 Phone Functions
1 Transfer Call — Press [Transfer Button], enter extension (no #), press Transfer again
2 External Transfer — Use # to transfer to school sites, elementary districts, or emergency extensions
3 Hold — Press [Hold Button]
4 Switch Between Calls — Use arrow buttons
🚪 Visitor Check-In (SafeVisitor)
1 Greet: 'How may I help you?' — Ask for ID
2 Log in to SafeVisitor: reception@hartdistrict.org / Hartdistrict2
3 Click Check In → scan back of driver's license (2D barcode)
4 Take photo → Select Reason & Person Visiting → Allow & Print Badge
5 District employees — Do NOT need badge. Have them sign the clipboard.
6 Do NOT let anyone into Special Ed dept unless explicitly authorized
📦 Mail & Packages
1 Unaddressed Mail — Place in yellow interdepartmental folder (top drawer near printer)
2 UPS/FedEx — Drop off next to double doors leading to back lobby
3 Sodexo packages — Call Lindsey x751 or Dana x752
4 Sodexo Payroll (red/gray plastic UPS pkg) — Contact Catherine Cardenas x754/750
5 CCR Packages — Email Claudia Balouzian
⚖️ Subpoenas / Summons
1 DO NOT accept or sign any subpoena or summons
2 Employee records — Contact Kristiana Molina x278
3 Student records — Contact Stephanie Migdol x203
4 If no one available — inform delivery person they may leave it at counter; you will notify
5 Log it on the tracker sheet in the labeled filing cabinet
📦 FedEx Mail Pickup
1 FedEx picks up mail in the morning — but it is not every day
2 People can leave their mail at the reception desk to be picked up
3 When FedEx arrives, give them all mail that has been left at the front desk
4 If FedEx does not come that day, keep the mail safe at the desk until they do
📄 W-2 Form — In-Person Drop Off
1 Receive the W-2 form from the employee at the front desk
2 Place the form inside a yellow interdepartmental envelope
3 Write PAYROLL on the envelope — no specific person needed
4 Deliver the envelope to the mail room
5 Note: W-2 requests by phone or email go to payroll@hartdistrict.org — employee must include full name, DOB, address, and last 3 digits of SSN
✅ Check Pick-Up
1 Verify ID before releasing any check

⚖️ California Workplace — Know Your Rights
This section summarizes the California Workplace Know Your Rights guide (CA Dept. of Industrial Relations). Use the search bar above to find specific topics.
🛡️ Worker Rights — General
Labor laws apply to ALL workers in California regardless of immigration status. It is illegal for your employer to retaliate against you for exercising your rights — including filing a complaint, asking about compliance with laws, or talking with others about their rights. Illegal retaliation includes firing you, reducing hours, or threatening to report you to immigration authorities.
rights worker california retaliation immigration
🔍 Immigration Inspections — Your Right to Notice
If your employer receives notice of an upcoming immigration inspection of I-9 forms or other employment records, they must post a notice informing workers within 72 hours of receiving that notice. (Labor Code Section 90.2)
immigration inspection i-9 notice 72 hours
📋 Protections Against Immigration-Related Practices
Under California law, employers cannot: refuse to accept genuine ID documents during I-9 process, misuse E-Verify, report or threaten to report you or your family to immigration authorities, or file false reports to police or government agencies because you exercised your rights. (Labor Code Sections 1019-1019.2)
immigration e-verify retaliation i-9 unfair
📞 Right to Designate an Emergency Contact
Your employer must allow you to provide emergency contact information and indicate if you want that contact notified if you are arrested or detained at work. If you are arrested at work and your employer has knowledge of it, they must notify your designated emergency contact. (Labor Code Section 1555)
emergency contact arrested detained immigration
✊ Right to Organize a Union
Most California employees have the right to organize, join, or participate in union activities. You can join with coworkers to request better conditions or raise work-related concerns about wages, hours, health and safety. You also have the right NOT to participate. It is illegal for your employer to interfere with, threaten, or retaliate against you for union or protected activities.
union organize collective bargaining protected activity working conditions
🚔 Rights When Law Enforcement Enters the Workplace
Law enforcement can enter PUBLIC areas (lobby, waiting room, parking lot) without a warrant. They need a judicial warrant signed by a judge to enter NON-PUBLIC areas (breakroom, employee restroom, workspace, employees-only areas). Your employer CANNOT give voluntary consent for immigration enforcement to enter non-public areas. Administrative forms like I-200 or I-205 are NOT judicial warrants.
law enforcement ice immigration agent search warrant police
🔎 Right to Be Free From Unreasonable Searches
If law enforcement asks to search you or your belongings, you have the right to say no UNLESS they have a judicial warrant specifically authorizing the search. If they conduct the search anyway — remain calm, do not physically resist, and do not run. (U.S. Constitution, 4th Amendment)
search warrant belongings 4th amendment immigration
🚶 Right to Be Free From Unreasonable Seizures
You are protected against unreasonable detention or arrest. Law enforcement must have reasonable suspicion before stopping and questioning you. You can ask: Am I being detained? or Am I free to leave? If not detained, you can walk away calmly. An arrest requires probable cause. You have the right to speak to a lawyer if arrested. You do NOT have to sign anything without speaking to an attorney.
detained arrested seizure 4th amendment stop question
🤐 Right to Remain Silent
Anything you say to law enforcement can be used against you in court. You have the right to remain silent, even if asked about your immigration status. Clearly state you wish to remain silent and request an attorney. Do NOT provide false information, false ID, or false documents — this is a federal offense with severe immigration consequences.
silent 5th amendment immigration status attorney lawyer
📹 Right to Record Law Enforcement
The public has the right to observe and record officers and government officials carrying out duties in public. If you record, stand a safe distance away and do not interfere with the officer. Physical obstruction or verbal escalation can put your safety at risk and may lead to criminal charges.
record film video 1st amendment officer public
⚖️ Access to Legal Representation
If arrested, you have the right to an attorney. If you cannot afford one, you can get a government-appointed attorney for a criminal case. If arrested by ICE or CBP for civil immigration violations, the government is NOT required to provide a lawyer, but you have the right to consult one before saying or signing anything. You can also ask to speak to your consulate.
lawyer attorney arrested ice cbp legal help
🏥 Workers Compensation
You have the right to workers compensation benefits if injured or ill because of your job. Benefits include medical care, partial wage replacement while recovering, and help returning to work. Contact: Lisa Arnone at x220 (Classified) or Kristiana Molina at x278 (Certificated). For general info: www.dir.ca.gov/dwc or call 1-800-736-7401.
workers comp injury accident ill job injury medical
